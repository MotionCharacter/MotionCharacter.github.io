Abstract

Recent advancements in personalized Text-to-Video (T2V) generation highlight the importance of integrating character-specific identities and actions. However, previous T2V models struggle with identity consistency and controllable motion dynamics, mainly due to limited fine-grained facial and action-based textual prompts and datasets that overlook key human attributes and actions.

To address these challenges, we introduce MotionCharacter, a human video generation framework designed for identity preservation and fine-grained motion control. Our approach introduces a motion control module that prioritizes action-related text while ensuring scene consistency, alongside Human-Motion, a new dataset utilizing large language models (LLMs) for detailed motion descriptions.

To streamline user control during inference, we parameterize motion intensity with a single coefficient. Furthermore, we combine ID and CLIP embeddings to maintain identity fidelity while enabling dynamic attribute adjustments and integrate mask and identity loss mechanisms to enhance consistency and detail fidelity. Overview

Recent advancements in personalized Text-to-Video (T2V) generation highlight the importance of integrating character-specific identities and actions. However, previous T2V models struggle with identity consistency and controllable motion dynamics, mainly due to limited fine-grained facial and action-based textual prompts and datasets that overlook key human attributes and actions.

To address these challenges, we introduce MotionCharacter, a human video generation framework designed for identity preservation and fine-grained motion control. Our approach introduces a motion control module that prioritizes action-related text while ensuring scene consistency, alongside Human-Motion, a new dataset utilizing large language models (LLMs) for detailed motion descriptions.

To streamline user control during inference, we parameterize motion intensity with a single coefficient, \( \mathcal{M} \). Furthermore, we combine ID and CLIP embeddings to maintain identity fidelity while enabling dynamic attribute adjustments and integrate mask and identity loss mechanisms to enhance consistency and detail fidelity. Extensive experiments validate MotionCharacter's effectiveness, demonstrating significant improvements in identity-preserving, high-quality video generation.
